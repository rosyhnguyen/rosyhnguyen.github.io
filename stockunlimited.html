StockUnlimited

  1. Product Design
Context

My project is to restructure the product line, change the pricing plan and redesign the website for StockUnlimited

My role

I worked as a product design and UX designer under the supervision of the Vice President.

Type &
Duration

Type: Coporate Work
Duration: 6 months

Problem Statement ‚öì

StockUnlimited is a subscription-based website that provides subscribers with unlimited downloads of stock digital content, such as stock graphics, vectors, images, and audio.

In the beginning, the content quality was low and limited with only 600k visuals. The price was pricey considering the low content standard. Most importantly, there was no key product to differentiate StockUnlimited from other subscription-based competitors.


Empathize with Users üëü

01. Survey

I sent a survey via email to customers who not recently download anything from their StockUnlimited subsriptions. The two major reasons for not using their accounts:

  1. (1) I only need a few images 34.8%
  2. (2) I have not been working on any creative projects for awhile 32.2%
Following are those reasons:
  1. (3) The price is expensive 16.8%
  2. (4) The quality of content could be improve3d 7.3%
  3. (5) The price is confusing 5.1%
  4. (6) It was difficult to search for what I want 2.6%
  5. (7) My account manager was not available for me 1.1%

2. Competitor Analysis

I picked out two biggest competitors: Envato Elements and Shutterstock.

Product: Envato has the most diversified product lines: graphics, templates, web templates, photos, footage while StockUnlimited focuses on vectors and audio. Shutterstock's key product is photo.

Price: StockUnlited has 2 different subscription plans: $9/month for images and $9/ month while Envato offers one flat price for all the site content, which makes users feel like it is StockUnlimited is more expensive.

Licensing Policy: Envato has the most simple licensing plans: one broad commercial license. StockUnlimited offers standard and extended license. Shutterstock follows almost the same licensing policy with StockUnlimited.

3. Focus group

I set up 2 different meetings. The first one was among our team of 3 people (two of my managers and I) sit down with staff from other brands to discuss Product, Price strategy for StockUnlimited. The second meeting was with legal team to find a way to streamline our licensing policy. I wanted to make ours as simple as Envato.

Ideate üîé

Product

I proposed to prioritise vectors as the key product while audio and photos should be the second priorities . This is because the niches for photos and audio was full while unlimited vector subscription plan still has room in the market.

Secondly, I worked with my manager to get more content contributors. We increased the library from 600k to 1+ million visuals.

Thirdly, we introduced download packs that work as lead products for potential and hesitant customers.

Price

Two separate subscription plans (audio and visual) were complicated; hence, we decided to merge image subscription and audio subscription into one single type of subscription: Unlimited content subscription.

Our three new pricing plans: $19 for monthly, $79 for annual (the key product) and $169 for 3 years access.

Licensing

The licensing policy was complicated. StockUnlimited had standard and extended license while Envato only has 1 broad commercial licence.

I still kept two licenses (standard and extended) but I simplified (and illustrated with infographcis) so that a customer without legal background can easily understand.
